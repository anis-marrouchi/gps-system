# Evidence Review — what's solid, what's shaky

Source video: Ali Abdaal, "Make 2026 the Best Year of Your Life (Evidence-Based)",
Dec 2025 — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D_KSR3S6W8I
The GPS framing itself is borrowed from Alex Hormozi (Abdaal credits him); the studies
are bolted onto individual steps. The composite "GPS method" has never been tested as
an intervention — "evidence-based" applies to components, not the package.

## Solid (rules in gps-adapted.md rest on these)

- **Progress monitoring** — Harkin, Webb et al. 2016, *Psychological Bulletin*
  meta-analysis: 138 studies, N≈19,951, d⁺=0.40. Effects larger when progress is
  physically recorded and made public/shared.
  https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/releases/bul-bul0000025.pdf
- **Implementation intentions (if-then)** — Gollwitzer 1999; Gollwitzer & Sheeran 2006
  meta-analysis, d≈0.65.
  https://www.prospectivepsych.org/sites/default/files/pictures/Gollwitzer_Implementation-intentions-1999.pdf
- **Specific + challenging goals** — Locke & Latham, ~50 years of studies. Caveat below.
- **Written goals + weekly accountability** — Matthews, Dominican University (N=267):
  ~70% vs ~35% success for those who wrote goals AND sent weekly progress reports to a
  friend. Single conference study, self-reported — directionally consistent with Harkin.
  https://scholar.dominican.edu/psychology-faculty-conference-presentations/3/

## Half-right (video oversells)

- **"Write your goals down → 42% more likely"**: the gain in Matthews came from
  accountability + weekly updates, not writing alone. The oft-cited Harvard/Yale
  "3% wrote goals" study never existed (debunked myth).
  https://ask.library.harvard.edu/faq/82314
- **Visualization**: Oettingen & Kappes 2011 — vivid positive fantasy *lowers*
  energization and subsequent success. Only mental contrasting (WOOP: wish, outcome,
  obstacle, plan) helps. Any "picture your best year" exercise without the obstacle
  step is contradicted by Abdaal's own cited researcher.
  https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S002210311100031X

## Shaky / unvalidated (system deliberately deviates)

- **Stretch goals without guardrails** — Ordóñez, Schweitzer, Galinsky & Bazerman 2009,
  "Goals Gone Wild": specific hard goals cause tunnel vision, unethical shortcuts, risk
  taking; and on novel/complex tasks, performance goals underperform learning goals.
  → hence mandatory anti-goals and the learning-goal override.
  https://www.hbs.edu/ris/Publication%20Files/09-083.pdf
- **12-week year** (Moran) — practitioner convention, no empirical validation of the
  horizon. Used here for rhythm only.
- **Survivorship bias** — Abdaal's advice generalizes from one high-agency, healthy,
  wealthy sample. Critics note it's light on burnout/sustainability research. The
  anti-goals (sleep, BP, family floor) are this system's correction.
