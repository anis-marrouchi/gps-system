# The Adapted GPS Method

Ali Abdaal's GPS skeleton (Goal → Plan → System), with each step corrected per the
evidence (see `science-notes.md`) and personalized to the owner's documented failure
modes (captured during onboarding into `data/profile.md`).

## Constraints that wrap the whole method

- **3 active goals max per 12-week cycle** — 1 business/work, 1 health, 1 optional.
  The cap is the single most important adaptation for prolific starters. New ideas →
  `data/parking-lot.md`, no exceptions mid-cycle.
- **12-week cycles** are a working convention (a horizon short enough to feel real),
  *not* validated science — don't defend them as evidence-based.
- **The system is the accountability partner** (or backs up a human one). People who
  deliver for clients but drift on self-set goals need rituals that behave like an
  external party: expect the report, notice absence, call out drift with dates and
  numbers.

## G — Goal

1. **Specific and measurable** (Locke & Latham). "Get fit" is rejected; a `metric`
   and `target` in frontmatter are required.
2. **Learning-goal override**: novel work (new market, new skill, new product motion)
   gets a *learning* goal — "run N experiments and document results" — not a
   performance number (Locke-Latham complexity moderator; Goals Gone Wild).
3. **Identity-framed why** (Bryan et al. 2011): one sentence, "the kind of person
   I'm becoming", not "I should".
4. **Anti-goals are mandatory** (`data/goals/anti-goals.md`): health guardrails,
   relationship floors, focus rules. A goal achieved by violating an anti-goal is
   scored as a miss — the guardrail against goal tunnel-vision (Ordóñez et al.).

## P — Plan

1. **3–5 milestones** mapped onto the 12 weeks, each with a date.
2. **Two 80% checks**, scored honestly by the owner:
   - *Does this plan make sense in theory?* (≥80% or simplify)
   - *Will I actually follow it, given my past behavior?* (≥80% or shrink it)
   The second check must reference the owner's real base rate from `data/profile.md`
   (e.g., burst-workers should structure plans as shippable bursts with checkpoints,
   not steady daily grind).
3. **Don't over-plan.** Milestones only; weekly reviews revise the route.

## S — System

1. **Tracking** (Harkin et al. — the strongest lever): one line per goal per day in
   `data/tracking/`, plus the weekly scorecard.
2. **WOOP pre-mortem, not visualization** (Oettingen): Wish → Outcome → Obstacles
   ("it's week 12 and this failed — what happened?") → Plan as if-thens.
3. **≥3 if-then implementation intentions** (Gollwitzer), at least one per obstacle
   class in `data/profile.md`.
4. **Accountability**: the weekly report is the Matthews mechanism. A configured
   human partner (`data/config.json → partner`) receives the weekly scorecard after
   the owner confirms. Solo mode is documented as weaker than the evidence supports;
   the quarterly reset re-asks the partner question.
